About Me

Born and raised in sunny Florida, I'm just old enough to remember the early days of dial-up internet - unplugging the home phone to connect to AOL, using AIM instant messaging, and watching in fascination as computers and the internet began weaving themselves into the fabric of practically everything we do.

That early exposure to technology's transformative power sparked a lifelong passion. At the Center for Advanced Technologies at Lakewood High School, I learned the fundamental building blocks of coding and hardware - getting my hands dirty with the nuts and bolts of how technology actually works. This foundation led me to St. Petersburg College, where I pursued an Associate of Science in Computer Programming, diving deeper into the logical structures that make our digital world function.

For the past 7+ years at Charter Communications, I've been living at the intersection of networks and innovation - supporting connectivity infrastructure while constantly integrating new technologies into daily operations. What excites me most is how the fundamentals I learned years ago continue to evolve, from basic network protocols to cloud computing, from simple automation scripts to AI-enhanced operations.
