About Me

Born and raised in sunny Florida, I'm a '90s kid from the dial-up days - when getting online meant kicking everyone off the phone. Those early days of trying to get connected were some of my first lessons in IT and networking.

That early exposure to technology's transformative power sparked a lifelong passion. At the Center for Advanced Technologies at Lakewood High School, I learned the fundamental building blocks of coding and hardware - doing labs and learning computer systems. This foundation led me to St. Petersburg College, where I pursued an Associate of Science in Computer Programming, diving deeper into the logical structures that make our digital world function.

For the past 7+ years at Charter Communications (Spectrum), I've been providing technical support for internet and voice services, helping customers resolve connectivity issues, DNS problems, router configurations, and service outages. This customer-facing role has given me extensive exposure to network troubleshooting concepts and real-world infrastructure challenges.
